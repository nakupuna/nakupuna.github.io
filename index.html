In 1997 a group of Hawaiian-American, island and mainland born seniors got together and formed an ukulele club. They called it the Na Kupuna Ukulele Club, as Na Kupuna means “elder” in the hawaiian language. Together they sing, play ukulele, and dance the hula to over 300 songs including “Aloha Oe”, “Blue Hawaii”, “Hawaiian Wedding Song” and “Kaulana Na Pua” or “Famous are the Flowers.” Over the years they’ve had countless performances all across Chicago, and as far as Hawaii. One of their most notable performances was when they were chosen by the City of Chicago to perform at the 2004 opening of Millennium Park.
